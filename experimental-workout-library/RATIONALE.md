# Rationale & Citations — 2026 Trends Pack

The physiological thinking behind each of the 7 workouts, and the sources.
Intensities are % of FTP.

| # | Workout | Energy system | The thinking (why it works / why it's trending) | Key prescription | Citations |
|---|---------|---------------|--------------------------------------------------|------------------|-----------|
| 1 | Rønnestad 30/15 | VO2max | Short 15s "floats" (not full rest) keep cardiac output and O2 uptake pinned near max, so you bank far more *total time >90% VO2max* than long 5-min reps — and it feels more doable. ~5% bigger FTP gain and larger VO2max gain vs 4×5min over ~10 wks. | 3 sets × 9–13 × (30s @110% / 15s @50%), 3 min between sets | Rønnestad et al. 2015, Scand J Med Sci Sports; TrainingPeaks; Spare Cycles |
| 2 | Seiler 4×8 | Upper threshold / VO2max | The highest-yield *single* HIT session in polarized training. Paced by "max sustainable session effort," not a fixed watt; maximizes time in the zone driving both threshold and VO2max. ~16% threshold / ~10% VO2max lift in 7 wks (recreational). Anchors the hard 20% of 80/20. | 4–5 × 8min @ max sustainable (~104–108%), 2min easy | Seiler & Tønnessen; Rønnestad & Hansen 2013; Wattkg; Roadman Cycling |
| 3 | Kolie Moore TTE | Threshold / time-to-exhaustion | Reframes FTP as a *duration* (30–70min at MLSS), not a 20-min number. Extend TTE by accumulating long at/near-threshold time and self-pacing ("float up" late) instead of ERG-locking. | 2 × 18–25min (→3×20) @ 96–98%, self-paced | Empirical Cycling (Kolie Moore); TrainerRoad forum; TrainingPeaks "Physiology of FTP" |
| 4 | Over-Unders | Lactate clearance / tolerance | Alternating just-under/just-over FTP trains the muscle to reprocess lactate *while still working hard* — the demand of paceline surges and TT attacks. The under is a working recovery, never coasting. | 3–4 blocks of 8–16min: 2min @95% / 2min @105% | TrainerRoad "Science Behind Over-Unders"; FasCat |
| 5 | Z2 + Sprints (Almquist) | Neuromuscular + aerobic base | 2020 finding: adding *maximal* 30s sprints to LOW-intensity rides (not a hard day) improved endurance 6 wks later and kept neuromuscular sharpness without raising load. Science behind "Z2 with sprints." | 90+min @60–65% + 2–4 sets of 3×30s all-out | Almquist et al. 2020, Front. Physiol.; Wattkg |
| 6 | Durability (fatigued threshold) | Fatigue resistance | Defining trend of 2024–26: fresh FTP says little about hour 4. Identical efforts before/after a long Z2 block expose and train late-ride power fade (decoupling after high kJ), now treated as an independent trait. | 2×10 @95% fresh → 60–150min Z2 → 2–3×10-12 @95–98% fatigued | Durability review (PMC); CTS; TrainingPeaks |
| 7 | Big-Gear Torque | Strength endurance / force | Low-cadence (45–60rpm) grinding raises pedal force, bridging gym strength to the bike. 2024 study: low-cadence *intervals* beat self-selected cadence for FTP/aerobic gains. Used by EF pros (Rüegg torque blocks). | 4–6 × 4–6min @88–95% at 48–60rpm, big gear | EF Pro Cycling; EVOQ.bike; Roadman Cycling |

**Coverage rationale:** only two sessions are VO2max-centric and they differ
structurally (micro-intervals vs long max-sustainable), so no energy system
dominates. The set spans force → threshold → lactate tolerance → VO2max →
durability → neuromuscular, which is why it periodizes cleanly into the 8-week
polarized block (see progressions/INDEX.md).
