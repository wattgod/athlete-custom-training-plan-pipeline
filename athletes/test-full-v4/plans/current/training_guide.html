Your gut is trainable. Over 24 weeks, you'll progressively increase your carbohydrate absorption capacity. This is essential — race-day fueling failures are usually gut failures.

8. Race Execution

Three-Act Pacing Strategy

Act 1: The Start (Miles 0-66)

Goal: Stay calm. Let the chaos pass.

  • Start conservatively — adrenaline lies
  • Find your rhythm in the first 30 minutes
  • Begin fueling by mile 10 at the latest

Act 2: The Work (Miles 66-150)

Goal: Steady execution. This is where races are made.

  • Maintain consistent effort — don't surge unnecessarily
  • Fuel relentlessly — every 20-30 minutes
  • Manage your mind — break it into segments

Act 3: The Finish (Miles 150-200.0)

Goal: Empty the tank. Leave nothing.

  • If you've fueled well, you can push here
  • Keep eating even if appetite drops
  • This is what you trained for — go get it
